Jiugang Bridge

A Project-Based Learning (PBL) Engineering Study

Project Overview

This website tracks our hands-on engineering journey creating a precise scale model of the Jiugang Bridge (舊港大橋) in Hsinchu. Through this project, we explored architectural design, structural stability, and aesthetic detailing.

Our Engineering Process

Step 1: Topic Definition

What We Did: We discovered that Old Port Island contains two bridges, and we chose to begin with Baidi Bridge as our initial focus.

Why Important: Without a defined topic, we could not initiate the project; therefore, we engaged in discussion and collaborated to move forward.

Step 2: Pivot to Jiugang Bridge

What We Did: At the outset of the project, we measured the dimensions of Baidi Bridge and found that its length was excessively long while its width was disproportionately narrow. Consequently, we decided to shift our focus to Jiugang Bridge as a more suitable subject.

Why Important: By measuring the actual length and width of the bridge, we determined that its dimensions were unsuitable for constructing a scale model.

Step 3: Foundational Construction

What We Did: We initiated the construction of the bridge model by developing its foundational base, incorporating both the supporting pillars and the structural towers.

Why Important: Establishing the base with pillars and towers was essential, as it provided the structural stability required to support all subsequent components of the bridge model.

Step 4: Painting Phase

What We Did: After completing the base structure, we moved to Jonah’s classroom to begin the painting phase, carefully applying vibrant colors to enhance both the visual appeal and overall presentation of the bridge model.

Why Important: Painting the bridge not only made the model look more professional and visually appealing but also helped distinguish different structural parts clearly.

Step 5: Deck Installation

What We Did: We securely attached and aligned the primary roadway deck across our established pillars and towers.

Why Important: The deck is the main platform of the bridge; installing it correctly ensured the model could hold weight and form a complete pathway.

Step 6: Adding Detailed Elements

What We Did: We added detailed elements such as safety railings, cables, and nearby landscape features to replicate the real Jiugang Bridge.

Why Important: Adding realistic details increased the accuracy of our scale model and made our presentation much more engaging.

Step 7: Structural Load Testing

What We Did: We conducted structural testing by applying small weights to the bridge deck to check its stability and strength.

Why Important: Testing allowed us to find any weak spots in our construction and fix them before the final presentation.

Step 8: Final Showcase Preparation

What We Did: We designed our exhibition poster, summarized our project data, and practiced our team presentation for the final showcase.

Why Important: Preparation and practice helped us clearly communicate our engineering process and teamwork to the audience and judges.
